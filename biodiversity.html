Preserving Natural Paradise

Costa Rica is a global leader in conservation. More than a quarter of its territory is designated as protected land, safeguarding incredible biodiversity ranging from jaguars to hummingbirds, coral reefs to cloud forests.

National parks and reserves span every ecosystem — from tropical dry forests in the northwest to rain-drenched jungles in the Osa Peninsula. These areas offer unforgettable opportunities for eco-tourism and scientific discovery alike.

Whether you're an adventurer, a nature lover, or simply curious, these parks invite you to experience Costa Rica's vibrant wildlife and landscapes with minimal environmental impact.

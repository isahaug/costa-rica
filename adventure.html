Adventure & Sports

Costa Rica is a thrill-seeker’s paradise. Go zip-lining through the Monteverde cloud forests, white-water rafting on the Pacuare River, or surfing the legendary waves of Tamarindo. Whether you're hiking a volcano or rappelling down a waterfall, adrenaline is guaranteed.

Horseback Riding in Costa Rica

Duration: Several hours

Type: Adventure

When: Varies by region

Costa Rica has a great love for horses, which began in the time of colonization with the development of agriculture and herding. Excursions on horseback allow travelers to explore beaches, forests, and mountains, often reaching spots inaccessible by vehicle.

Zip Lining in Costa Rica

Duration: 1–2 Hours

Type: Adventure

When: All year round

Zip lining is one of the most iconic adventures in Costa Rica. Glide through lush rainforest canopies and enjoy breathtaking views of volcanoes and rivers. Whether in Monteverde or Arenal, this activity offers a thrilling way to explore nature.

Hiking in Costa Rica

Duration: 1–5 Hours

Type: Nature

When: Varies by region

From easy walks to challenging volcano treks, hiking in Costa Rica means discovering waterfalls, wildlife, and tropical flora in every direction. With 26% of its territory protected, it’s a nature-lover’s paradise.

Diving in Costa Rica

Duration: Half-day or full-day

Type: Aquatic Adventure

When: Best during dry season (Dec–Apr)

Whether you’re a beginner or certified diver, Costa Rica offers world-class diving sites. Discover vibrant coral reefs, shipwrecks, and even hammerhead sharks around Isla del Coco, Caño Island, and Catalina Islands.

Kayaking in Costa Rica

Duration: 1–4 hours

Type: Nature & Water Adventure

When: Year-round

Explore mangroves, estuaries, and calm coastal bays by kayak. This peaceful adventure offers a close look at Costa Rica’s aquatic ecosystems—especially in regions like Tortuguero, Golfo Dulce, and Tamarindo.

White-Water Rafting in Costa Rica

Duration: Full-day or half-day

Type: Extreme Adventure

When: Peak in rainy season (May–Nov)

From beginners to thrill-seekers, white-water rafting in Costa Rica is a must. Tackle rapids on rivers like Pacuare, Sarapiquí, and Tenorio while enjoying lush jungle surroundings and waterfalls.

10 Unique Adventures You Can Only Have in Costa Rica

Ziplining

Fun Fact: Monteverde has the longest zipline in Latin America!
Where: Monteverde Cloud Forest.

Corcovado's Biodiversity

Fun Fact: Corcovado is home to 3% of the world’s biodiversity in just 0.03% of the Earth’s surface — it’s one of the most biologically intense places on Earth, according to National Geographic.

Where to do it: Corcovado National Park, Osa Peninsula

Surfing Both Pacific and Caribbean Coasts

Fun Fact: Costa Rica is one of the few countries where you can surf two different oceans in a single day — experienced surfers call it the "two-coast challenge."

Where to do it: Start in Puerto Viejo (Caribbean) and end in Jacó or Tamarindo (Pacific)

Hiking Active Volcanoes

Fun Fact: Costa Rica’s volcanoes are part of the Pacific Ring of Fire, and Arenal once erupted daily for over 40 years — you can still hike its lava trails today.

Where to do it: Arenal Volcano, Irazú Volcano, and Rincón de la Vieja

Soaking in Natural Hot Springs

Fun Fact: Costa Rica’s hot springs are heated by volcanic activity — and some, like Tabacón, flow directly from the base of Arenal Volcano at over 100°F.

Where to do it: La Fortuna, Arenal, Rincón de la Vieja

Chasing Majestic Waterfalls

Fun Fact: Costa Rica is home to hundreds of waterfalls — some hidden deep in rainforests and others glowing blue like Río Celeste, thanks to volcanic minerals.

Where to do it: La Fortuna, Bajos del Toro, Tenorio National Park

White-water Rafting on the Pacuare River

Fun Fact: The Pacuare River is ranked among the world’s top 5 rafting destinations, offering not just adrenaline-pumping rapids but also views of jaguars, toucans, and hidden waterfalls.

Where to do it: Pacuare River, Sarapiquí River, Tenorio River

Embarking on Scenic Road Trips

Fun Fact: Costa Rica's compact size allows you to drive from a Caribbean beach to a volcano to a Pacific sunset—all in one unforgettable day.

Where to do it: Route 32, Interamericana, Nicoya Peninsula, and Central Highlands

Scuba Diving in Pristine Waters

Fun Fact: Costa Rica is one of the few places in the world where you can spot hammerhead sharks, manta rays, and sea turtles in the same dive.

Where to do it: Caño Island, Catalina Islands, and Cocos Island (UNESCO site)

Jungle Yoga Retreats

Fun Fact: Many Costa Rican retreats are located within wildlife sanctuaries, so don’t be surprised if a monkey joins your morning meditation.

Where to do it: Nosara, Uvita, Santa Teresa

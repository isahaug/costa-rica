Biodiversity →

Costa Rica contains over 500,000 species, making up nearly 6% of the world's biodiversity despite its small size!

Culture →

Costa Rica has no standing army since 1948, and “Pura Vida” isn’t just a saying — it’s a national philosophy of life.

Safety →

Costa Rica is one of the safest travel destinations in Latin America, with friendly locals and a strong commitment to eco-tourism and visitor well-being.

General Travel Information

Spanish is the official language of Costa Rica. English is commonly spoken in tourist areas, but learning a few Spanish phrases can be helpful and appreciated.

No special vaccines are required. However, it's recommended to be up to date on standard vaccinations like tetanus, hepatitis A, and typhoid if you're heading to remote areas.

Costa Rica uses 110V electricity with standard U.S. plug types A and B. You won’t need an adapter if you're coming from the U.S. or Canada.

A 10% service charge is usually included in restaurant bills. Tipping extra is appreciated but optional. Taxi drivers don't expect tips but rounding up is common.

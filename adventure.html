Adventure & Sports

Costa Rica is a thrill-seeker’s paradise. Go zip-lining through the Monteverde cloud forests, white-water rafting on the Pacuare River, or surfing the legendary waves of Tamarindo. Whether you're hiking a volcano or rappelling down a waterfall, adrenaline is guaranteed.

Horseback Riding in Costa Rica

Duration: Several hours

Type: Adventure

When: Varies by region

Costa Rica has a great love for horses, which began in the time of colonization with the development of agriculture and herding. Excursions on horseback allow travelers to explore beaches, forests, and mountains, often reaching spots inaccessible by vehicle.

Zip Lining in Costa Rica

Duration: 1–2 Hours

Type: Adventure

When: All year round

Zip lining is one of the most iconic adventures in Costa Rica. Glide through lush rainforest canopies and enjoy breathtaking views of volcanoes and rivers. Whether in Monteverde or Arenal, this activity offers a thrilling way to explore nature.

Hiking in Costa Rica

Duration: 1–5 Hours

Type: Nature

When: Varies by region

From easy walks to challenging volcano treks, hiking in Costa Rica means discovering waterfalls, wildlife, and tropical flora in every direction. With 26% of its territory protected, it’s a nature-lover’s paradise.

Diving in Costa Rica

Duration: Half-day or full-day

Type: Aquatic Adventure

When: Best during dry season (Dec–Apr)

Whether you’re a beginner or certified diver, Costa Rica offers world-class diving sites. Discover vibrant coral reefs, shipwrecks, and even hammerhead sharks around Isla del Coco, Caño Island, and Catalina Islands.

Kayaking in Costa Rica

Duration: 1–4 hours

Type: Nature & Water Adventure

When: Year-round

Explore mangroves, estuaries, and calm coastal bays by kayak. This peaceful adventure offers a close look at Costa Rica’s aquatic ecosystems—especially in regions like Tortuguero, Golfo Dulce, and Tamarindo.

White-Water Rafting in Costa Rica

Duration: Full-day or half-day

Type: Extreme Adventure

When: Peak in rainy season (May–Nov)

From beginners to thrill-seekers, white-water rafting in Costa Rica is a must. Tackle rapids on rivers like Pacuare, Sarapiquí, and Tenorio while enjoying lush jungle surroundings and waterfalls.

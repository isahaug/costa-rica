Travel Tips for Visiting Costa Rica

✈️ Know Before You Go

Make sure your passport is valid for at least 6 months. Citizens from the U.S., EU, and most of Latin America don’t need a visa for stays under 90 days. Have proof of onward travel and hotel bookings — immigration may ask.

💵 Colones & Credit Cards

The official currency is the Costa Rican colón, but U.S. dollars are accepted in most tourist areas. Carry some cash for tips, taxis, and local markets. Credit cards are widely accepted, but rural shops may be cash-only.

🌦️ Packing for Paradise

Dry season (Dec–Apr) is sunny; rainy season (May–Nov) is lush but wet. Pack layers, a rain jacket, bug spray, and sunscreen. Don’t forget quick-dry clothes and a swimsuit — waterfalls await!

🚐 Getting Around

Public buses are budget-friendly, Uber works in San José, and taxis are reliable when official. For exploring beaches or volcanoes, a 4x4 rental is best. Download Waze — it’s the go-to navigation app here.

🙌 Respect the Rhythm

Greet others with a smile and a friendly “¡Pura vida!”. Costa Ricans value politeness and calm. Tipping is optional (10% is standard at restaurants). Avoid loud complaints — Ticos are chill, and you should be too.

🌱 Travel Green

Costa Rica is a sustainability superstar. Use a refillable bottle, skip single-use plastics, and stick to marked trails in parks. Choose locally owned hotels and guides — eco travel means pura vida for everyone.

📶 Internet & SIM Cards

WiFi is common in hotels and cafes. For better coverage in remote areas, grab a local SIM card (Kolbi, Claro, or Movistar). You can find them at the airport or any convenience store.

Your Safety in Costa Rica

Costa Rica is one of the safest destinations in Latin America. With friendly locals and a strong tourism culture, it offers a peaceful environment for travelers.

While major cities and national parks are generally secure, it’s still wise to take common travel precautions—like guarding personal items and staying alert in crowded areas.

In nature, always follow park rules and ask locals about sea currents or trail conditions. When prepared, Costa Rica is an incredibly rewarding place to explore confidently.

Top Safety Tips

  • ✅ Keep valuables secure and out of sight in public places.
  • ✅ Use official red taxis or authorized shuttle services.
  • ✅ Avoid swimming at unguarded beaches unless you know the current.
  • ✅ Carry a copy of your passport and keep the original in a safe place.
  • ✅ Use mosquito repellent in rural or forested areas.
  • ✅ If hiking or driving in remote areas, check weather conditions beforehand.
  • ✅ Emergency number in Costa Rica is 911.
